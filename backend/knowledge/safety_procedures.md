# Safety Procedures

## SP-101 Lockout/Tagout for Conveyor Work

Use this procedure before clearing jams, replacing guards, adjusting drive components, or reaching into a conveyor hazard zone. The floor supervisor verifies that all affected operators have been notified, the line has stopped, and the disconnect is placed in the OFF position. Each authorized worker applies a personal lock and tag before work begins.

Stored energy must be released before guarding is opened. Bleed pneumatic pressure, lower suspended loads, block gravity-fed sections, and verify zero motion by attempting a normal start from the local control station. Work may begin only after the zero-energy verification passes.

Before restart, confirm tools and parts are removed, guards are reinstalled, workers are clear, and each worker has removed only their own lock. The supervisor documents the return-to-service time in the shift log.

## SP-102 Hydraulic Leak Response

Treat any hydraulic leak as a slip, injection, and fire hazard. Stop nearby traffic, keep hands away from pinhole leaks, and establish a visible boundary around pooled fluid. Operators must wear safety glasses, cut-resistant gloves, and slip-resistant footwear before placing absorbent material.

If fluid contacts skin through a high-pressure injection, call emergency medical response immediately and report the suspected injection injury. Do not delay care to complete cleanup. If fluid reaches a drain, notify environmental response and block the drain when it is safe to do so.

Maintenance may inspect the source only after pressure is relieved according to the equipment lockout procedure. Cleanup is complete when absorbent material is removed, the floor is dry, and the area supervisor confirms the walking surface is no longer slick.

## SP-103 Hot Work Permit and Fire Watch

Grinding, welding, torch cutting, and other spark-producing work outside a designated hot-work cell require an approved hot-work permit. The permit check confirms combustible material is removed or shielded, fire extinguishers are available, gas cylinders are secured, and ventilation is adequate for the task.

A trained fire watch remains in the area during hot work and for 30 minutes after the final spark or flame. The fire watch may not perform unrelated tasks while assigned. If smoke, smoldering material, or uncontrolled sparks are observed, the fire watch stops the work and initiates the local alarm procedure.

Hot work may not start when sprinklers are impaired, combustible dust is visible on overhead surfaces, or the permit has expired. The supervisor files the completed permit with the shift safety record.

## SP-104 Chemical Spill Initial Actions

For an unknown chemical spill, isolate the area, avoid contact, and check the container label or safety data sheet from a safe distance. Do not mix absorbents or neutralizers unless the safety data sheet specifically allows it. Keep ignition sources away until flammability is known.

Small incidental spills may be cleaned by trained employees using the spill kit listed for that chemical family. Large spills, strong odors, visible vapor clouds, or symptoms such as dizziness require evacuation and emergency response notification.

The supervisor records the chemical name, estimated quantity, location, time discovered, and response actions. Waste from cleanup is labeled and staged in the designated hazardous waste accumulation area.
