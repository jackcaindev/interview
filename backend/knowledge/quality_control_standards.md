# Quality Control Standards

## QC-301 Dimensional Inspection Sampling

Use this standard for routine dimensional inspection of machined brackets, spacer blocks, and similar discrete parts. Inspect the first five pieces after startup or changeover, then inspect one piece every 30 minutes while the process remains stable. Increase sampling to five consecutive pieces after a tool change, machine alarm, or operator-reported abnormal condition.

Record nominal dimension, tolerance, measured value, gauge ID, operator, time, and disposition. A part is accepted only when every critical dimension is within the drawing tolerance and the measurement system is within calibration.

If any sampled part is out of tolerance, place product made since the last accepted check on quality hold and notify the floor supervisor and quality technician. Production may resume after containment and documented process correction.

## QC-302 Surface Defect Acceptance

Class A visible surfaces must be free of cracks, sharp burrs, loose coating, exposed base metal, and contamination that transfers to a clean wipe. Minor scuffs are acceptable only when they are not visible from one meter under normal inspection lighting.

Class B non-cosmetic surfaces may allow light handling marks and shallow scratches that do not affect fit, function, corrosion protection, or safety. Raised burrs on handled edges are not acceptable on any class of surface.

Reject and segregate parts with repeated coating bubbles, embedded debris, cracked corners, or burrs that could cut an operator or customer. Record defect type, count, lot number, and suspected process source.

## QC-303 Torque Verification Standard

Use this standard when verifying fasteners identified as torque-critical on the work instruction or control plan. The torque tool must be within calibration, set to the specified value, and verified with a torque tester at the start of the shift.

For startup verification, check the first three assemblies. During stable production, check one assembly every hour. If a fastener fails verification, place assemblies produced since the last passing check on quality hold and inspect according to the containment plan.

Do not average passing and failing torque results. Each checked fastener must independently meet the specified range. Record tool ID, setting, measured result, assembly serial or lot, operator, and verifier.

## QC-304 Nonconforming Material Hold

Use this standard when material is suspected or confirmed to violate a drawing, specification, control plan, or customer requirement. Identify the material with a red hold tag and move it to the designated nonconforming material area when movement will not damage evidence.

The hold record must include part number, lot or serial range, quantity, defect description, detection point, reporter, date, and immediate containment action. Material on hold may not be used, reworked, sorted, scrapped, or shipped without documented quality disposition.

Quality disposition options are accept-as-is by authorized concession, rework to approved instructions, return to supplier, scrap, or additional inspection. The floor supervisor confirms production has a clean restart point before releasing replacement material.
