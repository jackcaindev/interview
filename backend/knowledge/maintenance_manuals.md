# Maintenance Manuals

## MM-201 Conveyor Belt Tracking Adjustment

Use this maintenance instruction when a belt drifts toward one side, rubs a frame member, or trips a misalignment sensor. Inspect the conveyor for lodged debris, damaged rollers, uneven loading, and frayed belt edges before adjusting tracking. Correct mechanical damage before changing pulley or take-up settings.

Run the conveyor empty at jog speed when safe to observe belt travel. Adjust the take-up or tracking roller in small increments on the side toward which the belt is moving. Allow the belt to complete several revolutions after each adjustment before making another change.

After tracking is stable, run the conveyor under normal load and verify the belt remains centered through start, stop, and transfer conditions. Record the adjustment location and final observation in the maintenance log.

## MM-202 Hydraulic Press Preventive Maintenance

The hydraulic press preventive maintenance check covers reservoir level, filter differential indicator, hose condition, fitting seepage, ram alignment, guard interlocks, and abnormal pump noise. Perform the inspection at the scheduled service interval or after any unplanned pressure loss event.

Before opening a hydraulic line or replacing a filter, lock out the press and relieve stored pressure through the approved bleed point. Replace hoses with bulges, exposed reinforcement, cracked covers, or wet fittings that return after wipe-down. Use only the hose rating specified for the press.

After service, run the press through a no-load cycle and then a supervised production cycle. Confirm smooth ram travel, stable pressure, normal pump sound, and no new leaks before releasing the press to production.

## MM-203 Pump Motor Overheating Troubleshooting

Use this guide when a pump motor trips on thermal overload, smells hot, or shows surface temperature above the normal trend. Verify that the cooling fan is intact, ventilation slots are clear, and the motor is not covered by dust or packaging material.

Check supply voltage balance, current draw on each phase, coupling alignment, bearing noise, and pump rotation resistance. A motor drawing high current with normal supply voltage may indicate bearing failure, pump binding, or incorrect impeller clearance.

Do not repeatedly reset a thermal overload without identifying the cause. After corrective action, monitor current and temperature through one normal production cycle and document the readings.

## MM-204 Photoeye Sensor Cleaning and Alignment

Use this instruction when cartons are missed, false detects occur, or the controller shows intermittent photoeye input. Clean the emitter and receiver lenses with approved lens wipes. Inspect brackets for looseness, impact damage, and product buildup in the beam path.

Align the emitter and receiver until the signal indicator shows stable detection with the conveyor stopped and running. Confirm the sensor detects the smallest approved package and ignores normal background surfaces.

After adjustment, verify controller input status at the HMI and test three consecutive product passes. Record any bracket repair, cable replacement, or sensitivity change.
